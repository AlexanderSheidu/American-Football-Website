Super Bowl History and Facts

Super Bowl History

The Super Bowl is rich in history as it is the annual American football game that determines the champion of the National Football League (NFL). The game culminates a season that begins in the previous calendar year, and is the conclusion of the NFL playoffs. The first Super Bowl was held on January 15, 1967. Since then, it has become a significant event in American sports and culture, with traditions such as elaborate halftime shows, commercials, and parties.

Teams with the most Super Bowl wins

  1. Pittsburgh Steelers: 6 wins
  2. New England Patriots: 6 wins
  3. San Francisco 49ers: 5 wins
  4. Dallas Cowboys: 5 wins
  5. Green Bay Packers: 4 wins

Statistics about the Super Bowl

  • The first Super Bowl was between the Green Bay Packers and the Kansas City Chiefs, with the Packers winning 35-10
  • A total of 20 franchises, including teams that have relocated to another city or changed their name, have won the Super Bowl
  • The 1972 Miami Dolphins capped off the only perfect season in NFL history with their victory in Super Bowl VII
  • Only two franchises have ever won the Super Bowl while hosting at their home stadiums: the Tampa Bay Buccaneers in Super Bowl LV and the Los Angeles Rams in Super Bowl LVI
  • The most recent Super Bowl, LVIII, was between the Kansas City Chiefs and the San Francisco 49ers, with the Chiefs winning 25-22
